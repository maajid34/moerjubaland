Barwaaqo Project

Project Overview

The Rural Water Resilience Project, commonly referred to as the Barwaaqo Project, is a vital initiative aimed at significantly improving water infrastructure and resilience in Somalia's rural areas. The project is designed to address critical water needs, enhance the quality and availability of water resources, and build resilience against climate variability.

Scope: The project encompasses the development of new water sources, the rehabilitation of existing ones, and capacity building for local institutions to manage these resources effectively.

Phase Expansion: The Barwaaqo Project initially focused on regions including Southwest, Galmudug, Puntland, and Somaliland. The second phase has expanded to include Jubaland and Hirshabelle States, reflecting a broader commitment to addressing water needs across these additional regions.

Targeted Outcomes: The project aims to construct 7 new water points and rehabilitate 3 existing water points in Jubaland. It also includes comprehensive assessments to ensure effective planning and implementation.

Objectives

Project Objectives

  1. Enhance Water Access: Improve the availability and quality of water through the construction of new water points and the rehabilitation of existing ones.
  2. Build Resilience: Strengthen the capacity of communities to withstand and adapt to climate variability.
  3. Strengthen Institutions: Improve local institutional capacity for effective water resource management.
Coverage

Project Geographic Coverage

The project focuses on the following key locations in Jubaland State, based on their urgent water needs and strategic importance for regional resilience.

  • Dhobley
  • Diif
  • Afmadow
  • Karnusle
  • Janay Abdalle
  • Belet Hawa
  • Jirole
  • Dollow
